10. incomplete adjective clauses


Jawaban:

An adjective clause—also called an adjectival or relative clause—will meet three requirements:

First, it will contain a subject and verb.

Next, it will begin with a relative pronoun [who, whom, whose, that, or which] or a relative adverb [when, where, or why].

Finally, it will function as an adjective, answering the questions What kind? How many? or Which one?

20. Soal dialogue by phone dan jawabannya


Budi: “Dian, why didn’t you attend school today?”

Dian: “I’m sorry, Budi. I was feeling a little bit under the weather. I had to go to the hospital for a check-up.”

Budi: “What did the doctor say?”

Dian: “He said I had a fever, and then I needed to rest well if I want a quick recovery.”

Budi: “Oh, I see. I hope you get well soon, Dian. Please don’t forget to eat your medicines and take care of yourself.”

Dian: “Thank you very much, Budi.”


QUESTIONS:

1. Why was Dian absent in school?
2. What does Dian have to do in order to get well soon?

Jawaban:

Question Tags:

4. Mr.Yuda: You can use adjective to make descriptive text, can't you?

Penjelasan:

Jawaban dicetak tebal dan merupakan materi pembelajaran Question Tags (pertanyaan penegas) dalam Bahasa Inggris.

Berdasarkan kalimat utama yang berbentuk kalimat positif pada soal yang menggunakan Modal Verbs (kata kerja Modals) yaitu "Can" maka bisa kita simpulkan bahwa pertanyaan penegasnya harus menggunakan Modal Verbs yang sama, namun dalam bentuk negatif dan contracted (disingkat, misalnya can not = can't)

Maka struktur kalimatnya:

(+) Subject + Can + Verb-1 + Object + , + can't + Subject + ?
